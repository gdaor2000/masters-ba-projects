According to Visual Capitalist, which provides a comparison based on data from the UK Government’s methodology for greenhouse gas reporting, the CO2 equivalent emissions per passenger kilometer for different means of transport show that national rail emits around 41g CO2e per passenger km, and short flights have significantly higher emissions, up to 255g CO2e per passenger km​​. While these specific numbers vary from the ones we’ve chosen, they underscore the significant difference in emissions between air travel and rail, supporting the rationale behind your chosen values. The considerable variance in emissions between these modes of transport highlights the potential for significant emissions savings by opting for rail over short-haul flights (Bhutada, 2022) - https://www.visualcapitalist.com/comparing-the-carbon-footprint-of-transportation-options/.

Our World in Data emphasizes the environmental benefits of choosing trains over cars and domestic flights for medium-length distances, noting that trains are low-carbon ways to travel and can reduce your emissions substantially when chosen over other modes of transport​​ (Ritchie, 2023) - https://ourworldindata.org/travel-carbon-footprint. Although they don’t provide specific CO2e values per passenger km in the sections referenced, their discussion supports the general principle that rail transport is significantly more efficient in terms of carbon emissions compared to air travel.

Our selection of average emission factors (0.115 kg CO2 per km per passenger for flights and 0.025 kg CO2 per km per passenger for rail) and flights with locations that have a distance of less than 1000km between each other, appears to be a reasonable estimate that reflects the general consensus that rail travel is markedly more carbon-efficient than air travel. While exact figures can vary based on numerous factors including the type of train, the electricity source powering electric trains, and the specific aircraft and flight length, your chosen values are within the range of differences highlighted by the sources. These comparisons serve as a strong basis for promoting rail over air travel as a means to reduce carbon emissions in the transport sector.
